Geography and Climate Munnar is situated at an altitude of approximately 1,600 meters (5,200 ft) in the Western Ghats, spanning heights from 1,450 m to 2,695 m above sea level. It lies at the confluence of three rivers: Muthirapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundala, which gives the town its name, meaning "three rivers" in Malayalam. The climate is generally pleasant throughout the year: winter temperatures range from 6°C to 16°C, while summer ranges between 15°C and 25°C.
